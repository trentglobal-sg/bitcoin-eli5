Bitcoin Timeline

A list of all major events over Bitcoin's lifespan to today

Hashcash is proposed

Hashcash is a digital currency proposed by Adam Back with a proof of work mechanism based on hash functions. It is known as the predecessor to Bitcoin.

1997

May

2008

October

The Bitcoin Whitepaper is published

Published in 2008 by the pseudonymous inventor Satoshi Nakamoto, the Bitcoin Whitepaper is one of the most groundbreaking works of computer science since the internet, which lays the foundations of electronic peer-to-peer cash and its underlying blockchain technology.

First Bitcoin Block

Bitcoin Network goes live, and the first ever Bitcoin block kicks off with a reference to the headline of the day pertaining to the Great Financial Crisis of 2008.

The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks.

2009

January

2010

February

Bitcoin Markets established

The first place to buy or sell Bitcoins is established. With that, a price can be established.

The infamous pizza transaction

A famous transaction, where a forum user brought $25 worth of pizza with 10,000 Bitcoins which would have been worth $400m today.

2010

May

2010

November

Total marketcap crosses $1m.

The market cap is the market price multiplied by the total number of Bitcoins in existence crosses $1m in total.

First Bitcoin Block Halving

A halving event is when the reward for mining a block is halved, in this case from 50 BTC to 25 BTC.

2012

November

2013

October

Silkroad shut downs and its founder arrested

Silkroad was a dark web marketplace, which saw majority of its transactions in illicit drugs facilitated by Bitcoin is shut down by FBI and the founder Ross Ulbricht is sentenced to life imprisonment. 200k Bitcoins are siezed and auctioned by the US government.

Mt Gox Collapses

The largest exchange for Bitcoin collapses abruptly and a lot of investors who left their bitcoins in the exchange lost it all. In total, 744,000 BTC were stolen.

2014

February

2016

April

Steam accepts Bitcoin

The popular gaming platform Steam begins accepting Bitcoin for payment.

Second Halving Event

The block reward was decreased for the second time in Bitcoin's history, resulting in a new reward of 12.5 bitcoins per mined block.

2016

July

2010

November

Bitcoin Splits into Bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash

Due to a disagreement in how the network should progress, there is a split (hard fork) due to two disagreeing set of node operators.

10 Years Old!

After 10 long tumultous years, Bitcoin turns 10.

With the launch of Bakkt, backed by NYSE's parent company, the path is clear for instutions to allocate some assets to Bitcoin.

Third Bitcoin Halving

The reward per block mined on Bitcoin network was reduced to 6.25 BTC from 12.5 BTC.
