Scarcity

Only 21 million Bitcoins will ever be produced according to the chart below.

How are new Bitcoins made?

Bitcoins are newly made every block by a process called mining. The block reward is distributed to the first person who mines the block.

Hover on the following cards to learn more.

Initial Block Reward

50 BTC

The initial Bitcoin block reward was set to 50 BTC per block. Over time, as more blocks are mined, this number will decrease to zero.

Current Block time

The Bitcoin network dynamically targets a block time of 10 minutes, but because mining is a probablistic process, the current block time can vary depending on the amount of miners and the current difficulty.

Halving of Block Reward

Every 210,000 blocks

Every 210,000 blocks or around 4 years, the block reward is halved. Bitcoin has undergone its third halving.

Current Block Height

The current block height is the number of blocks that have elapsed since the Bitcoin network started.

Current Block Reward

The current block reward is 6.25 after the third halving event of Bitcoin. From an original 50, it was halved to 25, then to 12.5, and now to 6.25.

Current Block Reward

In USD

Taking the current market price of Bitcoin, this is the amount of money a miner who mines the winning block can get in USD.

Network Difficulty

The network difficulty is dynamically self adjusted to make sure that each block takes about 10 minutes to mine. It is dependent on the average time to mine the past 2016 blocks and will be adjusted if its too fast or too slow depending on a formula.

Electricity needed to mine a block

10% chance, in KwH

To calculate the amount of electricity, take the total network hash rate, and assume that your hardware is the commonly used Antminer S9 at 100 TH/s at 3050 W.

Cost of Electricity needed to mine a block

10% chance, In USD, US Rate

Comparison between two store of values

Bitcoin

Gold

There will only be 21 million Bitcoin at most, unless the network votes to increase it democratically.

Supply

The amount of gold is likely to keep increasing forever, especially when mankind explores space and start asteroid mining.

Your Bitcoins is easily stored encrypted, with paper, on disk, or even memorized in your brain, with zero storage costs.

Storage

Storing Gold is a logistical challenge, which requires constant security, physical space, which forces bulk gold buyers to buy ETFs instead.

Your private keys, your coins. There is no need to trust any party with your Bitcoins unless you decide to. The only thing you need to trust in cryptography.

Counterparty Risk

Because of the storage issues above, storing Gold requires trust. Trust that the government will not confiscate your gold, trust in the exchange that sells the gold ETF will store the gold properly, trust in your own security systems.

Bitcoins can be sent anywhere in the world through the internet without being blocked.

Censorship

Gold can't be easily sent overseas without getting governmental approval.

A Bitcoin transaction usually completes under 10 minutes.

Ease of Exchange

Gold can't be easily transacted, but money can.

Bitcoins can be transacted anonymously in any amounts, with the only paper trail being that on the blockchain.

Anonymity

Gold can't be easily transacted anonymously in large amounts, with most juristrictions requiring you to provide some form of transaction statement in person. For small amounts, gold is still easily anonymously transactable.

The Bitcoin network accepts changes democratically, with open votes and the opportunity to fork the network if there is disagreement.

Centralization

The US government and the banking cartels currently decide the policies for Gold.

Bitcoin exists in a space that is lightly regulated, which unfortunately leads to a lot of scams and losses to investors.

Regulation

Gold is a heavily regulated commodity that have real use cases like jewellery and in electronics.

Bitcoin is still mostly a speculative asset, but in recent years many investors brought it as part of their asset diversification. This means bitcoin is now an asset class of its own.

Store of Value

Gold is traditionally brought as a store of value, to hedge against inflation and protect value. It is not often used as a speculative instrument.

A software as a store of value is one of the greatest revolutions of the 21st century, secured by cryptography and democratically controlled.
